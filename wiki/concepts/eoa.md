# EOA (End of Authority)

**Source:** [[subset40]] | **Date:** 2026-05-07 | **Type:** concept

The **End of Authority (EOA)** is the location to which a train is authorised to move, as defined in SUBSET-026 §3.8.1.1 a) [¶50](http://localhost:8765/go.html#subset40-50). It serves as a key reference point for multiple engineering rules in SUBSET-040.

## Related Definitions

- **Danger Point**: A location beyond the EOA reachable without risk if no overlap is provided [¶51](http://localhost:8765/go.html#subset40-51).
- **Overlap (end of)**: A location beyond the Danger Point reachable without risk for a defined time [¶52](http://localhost:8765/go.html#subset40-52).
- **Stopping Point**: A location in rear of the EOA imposed by national operational constraints outside ERTMS/ETCS (e.g., in France: 5 m rear of signal) [¶53](http://localhost:8765/go.html#subset40-53).

## Engineering Rules Referencing EOA

- **Minimum distance from balise group to EOA (Level 1)**: Last balise giving MA or immediate level transition order shall be ≥ **1.3 m** in rear of EOA/LOA [¶65](http://localhost:8765/go.html#subset40-65).
- In-fill location reference must be in rear of current EOA [¶84](http://localhost:8765/go.html#subset40-84).
